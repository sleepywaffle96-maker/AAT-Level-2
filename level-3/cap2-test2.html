1. A firm's financial year end is 31 December. Electricity invoices are paid quarterly in arrears. Total cash book bank payments during the cycle are £5,400. The last quarter bill arrived in November (covering Aug, Sep, Oct) for £1,200. Compute the required adjustments.
Year-End Financial Component Balance Due (£)
Closing Accrued Expense Balance c/d (Current Liability)
Final Net Electricity Cost Overhead Transferred to SPL
Solution Note: Quarterly rate = £1,200 / 3 months = £400 per month. At 31 December, November and December remain unbilled (2 months × £400 = £800 Accrual). SPL charge = £5,400 (Bank) + £800 (Accrual) = £6,200.
2. Complete the regulatory framework descriptions regarding asset capitalization outlays.

The delivery cost of a new machine and its legal professional installation fees represent which must be capitalized. Conversely, a routine maintenance service plan on the same machinery is charged directly to the Statement of Profit or Loss.

Solution Note: IAS 16 dictates that any costs directly attributable to bringing an asset to its functional location and condition must be capitalized (Capital Expenditure). Regular tuning or servicing simply preserves value (Revenue Expenditure).
3. What technical double-entry is executed on the first day of a new financial period to handle the opening Prepayments b/f balance?
Solution Note: Opening prepayments must be stornati all'opposto (reversed) into the expense account on Day 1 to ensure the prepaid asset value matches current period consumption.
4. Gross Trade Receivables = £52,000. Opening Allowance b/f = £1,800. A customer balance of £2,000 is declared bankrupt at year-end and must be written off. Management directs a closing general allowance of 4%. Calculate the differential posting steps.
Receivables Evaluation Metric Required Balance (£)
Adjusted Trade Receivables Net Pool Base
New Required Closing Allowance Target Pool Balance
Net Allowance Variance Charge to SPL (Increase)
Solution Note: 1) Net debtors: £52,000 - £2,000 = £50,000. 2) Target provision: 4% of £50,000 = £2,000. 3) Incremental charge: £2,000 target minus £1,800 opening = £200 increase.
5. A debt of £450 written off as irrecoverable two years ago is unexpectedly recovered in cash. What is the correct year-end adjustment routing?
Solution Note: Since the historic client ledger record was completely closed, this windfall must be recognized as standalone operating income (Credit Irrecoverable Debts Recovered A/c).
6. On 1 November, a business pays an annual insurance premium of £7,200. The financial year-end is 31 December. Calculate the pro-rata monthly apportionment values.
Prepayment Adjustment Item Required Balance (£)
Prepaid Insurance Cost Deferral Balance c/d (Current Asset)
Current Year Insurance Expense Charge to SPL
Solution Note: Monthly insurance cost = £7,200 / 12 = £600. 2 months belong to this year (Nov, Dec = £1,200 charge). 10 months are prepaid for next year (10 × £600 = £6,000 Prepayment asset).
7. A business lets an office out to a tenant. At 31 December year-end, the tenant owes December rent of £950 which remains unbilled. Complete the classification routing.

This unbilled transaction represents which must be accounted for by the asset ledger and reported inside Current Assets.

Solution Note: Income earned but not yet documented must be recognized by debiting Accrued Income (Current Asset) and crediting the Rental Income account.
8. Under the strict instructions of the Prudence Concept, how should anticipated financial losses and potential future incomes be treated at year-end?
Solution Note: Prudence requires asymmetrical evaluation: incorporate anticipated risks/losses immediately (such as credit allowances), but bar unrealized gains.
9. Total rent received from a tenant during the cycle is £15,000. At 31 December, audit checks show that the tenant paid their January rent of £1,500 in advance. Complete the ledger metrics below.
Rental Ledger Variable Required Balance (£)
Closing Prepaid/Deferred Income c/d (Current Liability)
Net Rental Income matched and credited to the SPL Account
Solution Note: Revenue received in advance must be deferred: extract £1,500 to a temporary Current Liability account (Prepaid Income), leaving £13,500 matched to the current year's SPL.
10. A net increase variance is discovered between the new required general allowance target and the opening balance b/f. Select the correct double-entry ledger routing.
Solution Note: An increase in the general allowance represents a new anticipated operating cost overhead. It must be debited to the current period's expenses (Irrecoverable Debts A/c) and credited to the contra-asset valuation pool.
11. A specific allowance was made last year for a customer owing £800. This year, the customer pays the amount in full. Select the proper year-end ledger adjustment parameters.

The cash receipt is recorded normally in the cash book. At year-end, the specific allowance must be from the allowance account, which creates a inside the Irrecoverable Debts ledger stream to reflect the reduction in risk.

Solution Note: When a doubtful debt is paid, the specific provision is no longer required. It must be cleared out, resulting in a net decrease (Credit) in the current year's Irrecoverable Debts overhead account.
12. Why do companies prepare a 'Receivables Aging Schedule' (Aged Debtors Report) prior to calculating general provisions at year-end?
Solution Note: An aging schedule groups credit balances by time outstanding. Older debts carry higher default risks, requiring progressive provision tiers under prudent valuation guidelines.
13. Gross Trade Receivables at year-end = £62,000. Opening Allowance b/f = £2,500. A review reveals a required closing allowance target of £2,100. No specific bad debts are written off. Input the requested metrics.
Valuation Variable Required Balance (£)
Net variance credited to the SPL Account as income (Decrease)
Net Trade Receivables asset value exposed on the SoFP matrix
Solution Note: 1) Variance: £2,500 (Opening) - £2,100 (Target) = £400 reduction (Credit to SPL). 2) SoFP Display: Gross Receivables (£62,000) minus Closing Allowance Target (£2,100) = £59,900 net book asset value.
14. Under international framework rules, the Allowance for Doubtful Debts account is explicitly classified as what type of record?
Solution Note: The allowance is a contra-asset or asset valuation account. It carries a continuous credit balance used specifically to report receivables at their estimated net realizable value.
15. Final accounting review: Pre-adjustment Trade Receivables = £18,400. Opening Allowance b/f = £600. Year-end updates: write off a specific uncollectible balance of £400, then adjust the general allowance pool to exactly 5% of net credit debtors. Compute the final statement figures.
Year-End Statement Line Metric Required Balance (£)
Total Irrecoverable Debts overall charge to the SPL Account
Net Book Value of Trade Receivables exposed on the SoFP matrix
Solution Note: 1) Adjusted Pool: £18,400 - £400 = £18,000. 2) Target: 5% of £18,000 = £900. 3) Variance: £900 - £600 = £300 increase. Total SPL charge = £400 (bad debt) + £300 (increase) = £700. SoFP net value = £18,000 - £900 = £17,100.
