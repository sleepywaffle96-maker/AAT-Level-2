1. Classify the financial transactions according to the regulatory parameters of IAS 16 Property, Plant and Equipment.

The initial purchase cost of a delivery truck along with its delivery fees represents which must be added to the asset's cost on the SoFP. Conversely, the annual vehicle road licensing tax and fuel expenses constitute charged directly to the Statement of Profit or Loss.

Solution Note: IAS 16 dictates that capital spend includes all directly attributable deployment costs. Routine operation costs, licensing, and fuel are operational overheads (Revenue Expenditure).
2. On 1 April, an entity buys factory equipment for £24,000. It has an estimated residual value of £4,000 and a 5-year Useful Economic Life (UEL). The accounting year ends on 31 December. The corporate policy applies a strict pro-rata monthly depreciation basis. Compute the Year 1 statement variables.
Year 1 Financial Metric Required Balance (£)
Annual Depreciation Expense overhead charged to current SPL
Closing Net Book Value (NBV) exposed on the SoFP matrix
Solution Note: Full annual rate: (£24,000 - £4,000) / 5 years = £4,000/annum. Owned for 9 months (April to December): £4,000 × (9/12) = £3,000 overhead. Year 1 Closing NBV: Historic Cost (£24,000) - Accum. Depreciation (£3,000) = £21,000.
3. Under accounting frameworks, what is the primary structural objective of recording an annual depreciation entry for non-current assets?
Solution Note: In accordance with the Matching Principle, depreciation systematically dilutes capital expenditures into operational exercises over time, ignoring open market re-sale fluctuations.

6. A business policy explicitly mandates: "Full year's depreciation in the year of acquisition, none in the year of disposal". An asset is bought 2 weeks before the year-end closing date. How much depreciation must be charged?
Solution Note: Under a full-year acquisition rule, chronology is completely ignored. The bookkeeper allocates a complete 12-month overhead block regardless of the specific purchase date.

Solution Note: Reducing balance mathematics apply fixed percentage rates strictly to the changing Net Book Value (NBV). Residual scrap fields are fully ignored during these periodic adjustments.

10. A credit customer defaults permanently due to formal insolvency. What ledger double-entry must be executed to record this specific debt write-off?
Solution Note: Specific bankruptcies reduce company assets. The bookkeeper credits the Trade Receivables Control (SLCA) and transfers the loss to operating overheads by debiting Irrecoverable Debts.
