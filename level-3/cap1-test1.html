1. Complete the operational statements regarding year-end adjustments classifications according to IAS 1 framework parameters.

An overhead expense that has been consumed by the entity during the current period but remains unbilled at the year-end date constitutes an which must be systematically reported as a . Conversely, an expense paid in advance covering next year's service window represents a classified as a .

Solution Note: Under IAS 1 framework parameters, accrued expenses reflect unbilled legal obligations owed to third parties (Current Liabilities). Prepaid expenses reflect controlled economic values paid upfront to be enjoyed in future cycles (Current Assets).
2. A business pays its telephone overheads quarterly in arrears. The financial year ends on 31 December. The total bank payments made during the year stand at £4,200. The last quarter bill arrived in November (covering Aug, Sep, Oct) totaling £900. Complete the fundamental adjustment cells below.
Accounting Variable Item Required Balance (£)
Year-End Accrued Expense Closing Balance (c/d)
Total Net Telephone Expense Charge to SPL Account
Solution Note: The quarterly rate is £900 / 3 months = £300/month. For Nov and Dec, the overhead is unbilled (2 months × £300 = £600 Accrual). Total profit or loss expense charge is: £4,200 (Bank) + £600 (Accrual) = £4,800.
3. On 1 October, a corporate entity settles an annual insurance policy premium of £4,800. The corporate financial year ends on 31 December. Calculate the missing values of the adjusted variables below.
Accounting Adjustment Metric Required Balance (£)
Prepaid Expense Adjustment Balance (c/d Current Asset)
Net Insurance Expense Overhead Charge to SPL Account
Solution Note: The policy is £4,800 / 12 = £400/month. 3 months apply to the current year (Oct, Nov, Dec = £1,200 charge). 9 months apply to next year (Jan-Sep = £3,600 Prepayment asset).
4. Which accounting concept dictates that expenses must be matched against the revenues they help generate in the exact same reporting period, rather than when the physical cash transaction occurs?
Solution Note: The Accruals Basis (or Matching Principle) under IAS 1 requires costs and revenues to be logged in the period they occur, independent of cash movements.
5. At 31 December year-end, a firm extracts a gross Trade Receivables balance of £30,500 and an opening Allowance b/f of £1,100. A specific customer debt of £500 must be written off as irrecoverable. Management directs that a closing general allowance of 5% must be established. Complete the adjusted values below.
Receivables Ledger Valuation Step Required Balance (£)
Adjusted Net Receivables Pool for Allowance calculations
New Required Closing Allowance Target Pool Balance
Net Differential Allowance Variance posted to SPL (Increase)
Solution Note: Adjusted Pool: £30,500 - £500 = £30,000. Target: 5% of £30,000 = £1,500. Variance increase to SPL: £1,500 (Target) - £1,100 (Opening b/f) = £400.
