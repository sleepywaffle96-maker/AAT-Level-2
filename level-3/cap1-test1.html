2. A business pays its telephone overheads quarterly in arrears. The financial year ends on 31 December. The total bank payments made during the year stand at £4,200. The last quarter bill arrived in November (covering Aug, Sep, Oct) totaling £900. Complete the fundamental adjustment cells below.
Accounting Variable Item Required Balance (£)
Year-End Accrued Expense Closing Balance (c/d)
Total Net Telephone Expense Charge to SPL Account
Solution Note: The quarterly rate is £900 / 3 months = £300/month. For Nov and Dec, the overhead is unbilled (2 months × £300 = £600 Accrual). Total profit or loss expense charge is: £4,200 (Bank) + £600 (Accrual) = £4,800.
3. On 1 October, a corporate entity settles an annual insurance policy premium of £4,800. The corporate financial year ends on 31 December. Calculate the missing values of the adjusted variables below.
Accounting Adjustment Metric Required Balance (£)
Prepaid Expense Adjustment Balance (c/d Current Asset)
Net Insurance Expense Overhead Charge to SPL Account
Solution Note: The policy is £4,800 / 12 = £400/month. 3 months apply to the current year (Oct, Nov, Dec = £1,200 charge). 9 months apply to next year (Jan-Sep = £3,600 Prepayment asset).
4. Which accounting concept dictates that expenses must be matched against the revenues they help generate in the exact same reporting period, rather than when the physical cash transaction occurs?
Solution Note: The Accruals Basis (or Matching Principle) under IAS 1 requires costs and revenues to be logged in the period they occur, independent of cash movements.

8. On the first day of a new financial period, what technical bookkeeping procedure must be performed on the closing balances of Accruals and Prepayments?
Solution Note: Opening Reversal Entries are mandatory on Day 1 of the new period to clear temporary adjustment accounts and prevent double-counting when new payments emerge.
9. A business has a net Trade Receivables pool of £20,000 at year-end. The opening Allowance for Doubtful Debts b/f stands at £1,200. Management decides to set a new allowance target of 4% on receivables. Calculate the ledger variance metrics.
Receivables Valuation LineRequired Balance (£)

Solution Note: Target allowance = £20,000 × 4% = £800. Comparing with opening balance (£1,200), the allowance decreased by £400. This variance represents an over-provision released back as a credit entry in the SPL.
10. Under the requirements of international financial frameworks, what is the ultimate accounting objective of creating a general allowance for doubtful debts?
Solution Note: In compliance with the prudence concept, an allowance ensures that the assets are not overstated, recording trade receivables at their net expected realizable cash collection value.

12. A debt written off as irrecoverable in a previous financial period is suddenly collected in cash during the current year. How should this be treated?
Solution Note: Since the individual ledger record was already closed out, the cash input must be recognized as specific standalone operating income (Credit Irrecoverable Debts Recovered A/c).
13. A company receives rental income from a sub-let property. Total cash bank receipts during the year are £12,000. At year-end on 31 December, the tenant has prepaid rent for January of the next year totaling £1,000. Complete the adjustment line cells below.
Rental Income Variable Required Balance (£)
Year-End Prepaid/Deferred Income Balance c/d (Liability)
Total Net Rental Income credited to the SPL Account
Solution Note: Rent received in advance must be extracted to prevent profit inflation. Closing Prepaid Income = £1,000 (Current Liability). Net match sent to SPL = £12,000 - £1,000 = £11,000.
14. Why does the general allowance mechanism apply a risk percentage to potential defaults while ignoring potential unexpected windfall revenues?
Solution Note: The principle of Prudence mandates asymmetrical caution: recognize liabilities and losses as soon as they are realistically anticipated, but never anticipate revenues.
