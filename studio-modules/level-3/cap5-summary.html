Chapter 5: Professional Ethics Critical Summary

🎯 THE PROFESSIONAL ACCOUNTANT'S CORE RESPONSIBILITY FRAMEWORK:

1. The Public Interest: An accountant's overriding duty is to protect the public interest, which ranks above client or employer loyalty.
2. POCA Mandate: Failure to disclose money laundering = Up to 5 years imprisonment.
3. Tipping Off Mandate: Alerting a suspect under SAR investigation = Up to 5 years imprisonment.

Statutory UK Legislation Act Core Impact on Accounting Databases Regulatory Penalty Compliance Focus
Proceeds of Crime Act (POCA) Mandates Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and automatic SAR creation. Criminal prosecution for non-disclosure or tipping off.
Data Protection Act / GDPR Protects confidentiality; bars unencrypted storage of payroll or client accounts. Massive corporate fines calculated as a percentage of global turnover.
Bribery Act Criminalizes corporate failure to prevent commercial bribery within workflows. Unlimited corporate fines and director disqualification structures.
🎯 STRATEGIC ETHICS EXAM-DAY CHECKLIST:
  • Identify the Override: When an exam question involves suspected money laundering or tax evasion, confidentiality is legally overridden by the Proceeds of Crime Act. You must report, not stay silent.
  • The Anonymity Consultation Rule: If consulting the AAT technical support line about an internal dilemma, you must keep all client names anonymous to protect basic data protection compliance laws.
  • Safeguard or Resign: If internal corporate directors refuse to log a transaction adjustment or alter fraudulent data entries, and safeguards fail, the final professional step is formal **Resignment** from the engagement.
