Management Accounting Techniques (MATC)

AAT Syllabus Context: This unit focuses on role-specific knowledge required to provide management with information for planning, control, and decision-making regarding costs, inventory, and budgeting.

1. Cost Classification and Behaviour

To provide relevant data to stakeholders, management accountants must classify costs based on their behaviour in relation to changes in activity levels:

Classification by Nature

  • Direct Costs: Expenses that can be directly and fully traced to a specific cost unit (e.g., raw materials, direct factory wages).
  • Indirect Costs (Overheads): Expenses that cannot be directly traced to a single unit (e.g., factory rent, supervisor salaries).

Classification by Behaviour

  • Fixed Costs: Remain constant in total over a specific range of activity (relevant range), but decrease per unit as production rises (e.g., rent).
  • Variable Costs: Change in direct proportion to production volume, remaining constant per unit (e.g., raw materials).
  • Semi-Variable Costs: Contain both a fixed and a variable element (e.g., electricity bills with a standing charge plus usage rate). Analysts split these using the High-Low Method.
  • Stepped Fixed Costs: Fixed within a certain level of activity but jump to a higher level once capacity is reached (e.g., hiring an extra warehouse).

2. Costing Methodologies

Organisations use different costing methods depending on their inventory valuation strategies and production frameworks.

Absorption Costing

This approach allocates both variable and fixed manufacturing costs to individual cost units. It satisfies external reporting requirements (IAS 2) by ensuring closing inventory values carry a share of fixed factory overheads. The process involves three distinct steps:

  • Allocation: Charging whole cost items directly to cost centres.
  • Apportionment: Sharing indirect costs across multiple cost centres using a fair baseline (e.g., square metres for rent).
  • Absorption: Recovering overheads into product units using a predetermined Overhead Absorption Rate (OAR).

Marginal Costing

This framework assigns only variable costs to products. Fixed overheads are treated as period costs and are expensed entirely in the period they occur. Inventory is valued strictly at marginal (variable) cost. The key metric generated is Contribution (Sales Revenue less Variable Costs), which assists managers in short-term decision-making, special pricing options, and break-even analysis.

3. Inventory Control Frameworks

Proper physical and financial valuation of inventory directly impacts profit and asset visibility. AAT students must master the following mechanisms:

  • FIFO (First In, First Out): Assumes the oldest inventory items are sold first. Closing inventory values reflect current market prices.
  • LIFO (Last In, First Out): Assumes the newest items are sold first. (Note: Not permitted under IAS 2, but used for internal benchmarking).
  • AVCO (Weighted Average Cost): Recalculates the average unit price either continuously after each delivery or periodically at month-end.
