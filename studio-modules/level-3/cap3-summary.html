Chapter 3: Costing Summary & Quick Cheat Sheet

🎯 CORE MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTING EQUATIONS:

1. High-Low Variable Rate = (Highest Activity Cost - Lowest Activity Cost) / (Highest Units - Lowest Units)
2. Predetermined OAR = Predetermined Budgeted Overheads Pool / Forecasted Activity Base Hours
3. Under/Over Absorption Variance = Predetermined Absorbed Overheads - Actual Overheads Incurred

Costing Variance Scenario Mathematical Status Condition Impact on the Operating Performance Accounts
Over-Absorption of Overheads Absorbed Overheads (OAR × Actual Hours) > Actual Overheads Incurred Product cost was overstated; acts as an adjustment credit row to increase reported net profit.
Under-Absorption of Overheads Absorbed Overheads (OAR × Actual Hours) < Actual Overheads Incurred Product cost was understated; acts as an additional operating debit overhead charge to cut profit.
🎯 STRATEGIC COSTING CHECKLIST:
  • Absorption Bases Selection: If a production department is labor-intensive, always utilize direct labor hours as the OAR base. If it is automated or highly mechanized, switch the OAR denominator base strictly to machine hours.
  • Service Center Clearance: When executing apportionment steps, ensure service departments are completely cleared down. Their final balances in the allocation grid rows must always read zero.
  • Continuous AVCO Balancing: When updating stack ledger sheets, re-average the valuation price only upon receipts. Inventory issues simply drain stock out using the current active average price.
