📊 SCHEMA 1: IESBA ETHICAL DILEMMA ESCALATION PIPELINE
[ STEP 1: IDENTIFY THE BREACH ] ---------> Audit the transaction facts against fundamental principles (Is Integrity, Objectivity or Confidentiality threatened?) | v [ STEP 2: INTERNAL ESCALATION ] ---------> Report the concern internally to line managers or directors (Bypass any specific individual involved in the breach) | v [ STEP 3: INDEPENDENT ADVICE ] ----------> Consult the AAT Technical Ethics Helpline or legal advisors (Keep data anonymous to protect client confidentiality) | v [ STEP 4: FINAL DISENGAGEMENT ] ---------> If internal fraud persists, formalize your **Resignation** and submit a report to relevant authorities (HMRC/NCA/AAT).
📋 SCHEMA 2: THE THREATS AND COMPLIANCE SAFEGUARDS MATRIX
Ethical Conflict Scenario Identified Core Threat Category Mandatory Safeguard / Action Required
A client offers an expensive gift or lavish hospitality to the lead accountant. Self-Interest Threat Politely decline the gift immediately. Implement a strict firm policy regarding hospitality limits.
The firm is asked to perform the year-end audit for a financial client whose books were prepared by the same team. Self-Review Threat Assign a completely separate team of professionals to handle the audit, or refuse the audit engagement entirely.
The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) pressures a junior accountant to overstate asset values to secure a bank loan. Intimidation Threat Document all correspondence. Escalate the matter to the internal audit committee, or whistleblow if blocked.
