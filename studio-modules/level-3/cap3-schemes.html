📊 SCHEMA 1: THE FOUR-STAGE OVERHEAD ABSORPTION MATRIX STRATIFICATION
[ STAGE 1: EXPENSE GROUP ALLOCATION ] ---> Assign direct line costs to targets (e.g., Canteen Expenses -> Canteen A/c) | v [ STAGE 2: SHAREABLE APPORTIONMENT ] ---> Split joint items across centers via math metrics (Factory Rent -> Floor Area SqM | Power -> Machine Kilowatt Hours) | v [ STAGE 3: RECIPROCAL REAPPORTION ] ---> Wipe out service departments. Transfer Canteen & Maintenance balances into productive departments based on service use rates. | v [ STAGE 4: PREDETERMINED ABSORPTION ] ---> Compute OAR Pool Rates. Absorb overheads into individual units using actual direct labor hours or mach hours worked.

📈 SCHEMA 2: STOCK CONTROL SHEET VALUATION MATRIX (INFLATIONARY CLIMATE)
================================================================================================== VALUATION STRUCTURE ANALYSIS (WHEN UNIT REPLACEMENT COSTS ARE CONTINUOUSLY RISING) ================================================================================================== METHODOLOGY | PRODUCTION UNIT ISSUE CHARGES | CLOSING BALANCE VALUATION ON BALANCE SHEET -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- FIFO | Lower Costs. | Higher Valuation. | (Production takes older cheaper stock) | (Stock remaining matches newest costly lots) | -> Boosts current short-term profits. | -> Inflates inventory current asset metrics. ------------+----------------------------------------+-------------------------------------------- AVCO | Smoothed, Weighted Average Cost. | Smoothed, Weighted Average Cost. | (Blends old and new buying prices) | (The average unit value rolls seamlessly) | -> Dampens raw pricing jumps. | -> Equalizes asset balance fluctuations. ================================================================================================== *Note: FIFO provides a modern balance sheet asset view, while AVCO yields a highly stable profit view.
