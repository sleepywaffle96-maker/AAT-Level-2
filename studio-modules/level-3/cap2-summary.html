Chapter 2: Critical Summary & Depreciation Cheat Sheet

🎯 KEY DEPRECIATION FORMULAS & BALANCING EQUATIONS:

1. Straight-Line Annual Charge = (Historic Cost - Estimated Residual Value) / Useful Economic Life
2. Net Book Value (NBV) Matrix = Historic Asset Cost - Cumulative Accumulated Depreciation
3. Reducing Balance Annual Charge = Current Net Book Value (NBV) × Management Depreciation Rate (%)

Accounting Metric Straight-Line Method Reducing Balance Method
Calculation Base Pool Original Historic Cost (Minus Residual Value if provided). Net Book Value (Cost minus existing Accumulated Depreciation).
Annual Overhead Expense Behavior Stays identical and constant every full financial year. Decreases progressively every year as the asset ages.
Treatment of Residual Value Deducted upfront before applying percentage or UEL division. Completely ignored during annual calculation steps.
🎯 STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT DAY CHECKLIST:
  • Policy Verification: Check if the question applies a pro-rata monthly rule or a full-year policy on acquisitions. Misapplying the timing apportionment rules will cause zero points to be awarded for the calculation row.
  • Account Split Isolation: Ensure that asset cost adjustments and accumulated depreciation pools remain strictly in separate ledgers. Never merge or offset these balances during initial journal entries.
  • Overhead Routing: Remember that the Depreciation Expense account is a nominal record cleared down to zero at year-end, while the Accumulated Depreciation account carries its balances forward to the next financial year as a valuation reducer in the SoFP.
