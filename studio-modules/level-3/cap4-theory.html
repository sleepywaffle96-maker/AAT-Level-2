Tax Processes for Businesses (TPFB)

AAT Syllabus Context: This chapter covers the administration, calculations, regulations, and submission requirements for Value Added Tax (VAT) in compliance with HMRC guidelines.

1. Legislation and VAT Principles

Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax charged on taxable supplies of goods and services in the UK. Businesses act as collecting agents for HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC).

Types of Supplies

  • Taxable Supplies: Subject to VAT at standard rate (20%), reduced rate (5%), or zero rate (0%).
  • Exempt Supplies: Outside the VAT system completely (e.g., insurance, finance, education). No VAT is charged, and input VAT cannot be recovered.

Registration Framework

  • Compulsory Registration: Required if taxable turnover exceeds the historical threshold (£90,000) in a rolling 12-month period, or is expected to exceed it in the next 30 days.
  • Voluntary Registration: Permitted if turnover is below the threshold, allowing businesses to reclaim input VAT on expenses.

2. Special VAT Schemes

HMRC offers several optional accounting arrangements designed to streamline small business cash flow and financial reporting obligations:

Cash Accounting Scheme

Under standard accounting, VAT is recorded based on invoice dates. Under the Cash Accounting Scheme, VAT is accounted for based on actual payment dates. This protects businesses from paying VAT to HMRC on bad debts before receiving payment from clients.

Annual Accounting Scheme

Allows businesses to submit only one VAT return per year instead of four quarterly returns. Advance interim payments are made throughout the year, followed by a final balancing payment or refund claim.

Flat Rate Scheme

Designed to simplify bookkeeping. Businesses apply a fixed, industry-specific percentage directly to their gross turnover to determine the VAT liability. They do not reclaim input VAT on everyday purchases, except for capital assets costing over £2,000.

3. The VAT Return and Penalties

Registered entities must submit their VAT data digitally using Making Tax Digital (MTD) software. Returns are normally due 1 calendar month and 7 days after the end of the VAT period.

Failure to submit or pay on time triggers HMRC’s points-based penalty system and financial interest surcharges on overdue amounts.
