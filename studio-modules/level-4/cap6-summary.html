DAIF Formula Cheat Sheet & Reference

Essential core mathematical equations for interpretation exams.

1. Primary Profitability Equations

Return on Capital Employed (ROCE %)
(Operating Profit / [Equity + Non-Current Liabilities]) x 100
Asset Turnover Ratio
Revenue / (Equity + Non-Current Liabilities) [expressed as times]

2. Financial Gearing & Leverage Risks

Capital Gearing % Method
(Long-Term Debt / [Equity + Long-Term Debt]) x 100
Interest Cover Ratio
Operating Profit / Finance Costs [expressed as times]

Values below 2.0 indicate significant risk of default during structural downturns.

3. Total Working Capital Net Cycle

Net Cycle Days = Inventory Days + Receivables Days - Payables Days

4. Ratio Interpretation Rules

  • Operating Profit Baseline: Always evaluate profit before charging interest expenses and taxation lines to maintain cross-company comparability.
  • Overtrading Symptoms: Rapidly expanding revenue alongside dropping liquidity ratios, surging current bank overdraft balances, and ballooning receivables days.
  • Window Dressing: Making temporary transactional changes just before year-end to artificially distort accounting health metrics (e.g., executing emergency short-term cash payments to suppliers to polish liquidity benchmarks).
