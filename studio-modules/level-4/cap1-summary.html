AMAC Budgeting Core Revision Formulas

Mathematical forecasting models and time-series formulas for exam operations.

1. Linear Regression Equations (Trend Forecasting)

The Straight Line Formula
y = a + bx

y = Total Forecasted Value | a = Intercept (Fixed baseline) | b = Gradient (Variable trend rate) | x = Time Period Number

2. Production Inventory Adjustment Matrix

Required Production (Units) = Forecasted Sales Units + Closing Stock Target - Opening Stock b/f

3. Raw Materials Purchase Allocation

Purchases Needed (£) = [(Production Units x Unit Material Spec) + Target Closing Inventory - Opening Stock] x Cost per Unit

4. Strategic Planning Cheat Sheet

  • Principal Budget Factor: The initial structural constraint (usually market demand or production capacity) that determines the entire sequence of master budget creation.
  • Budgetary Slack: Deliberate overestimation of expenses or underestimation of revenues by operational managers to make targets easier to achieve.
  • Participation Benefit: Bottom-up budgeting improves staff motivation and increases local target accuracy but consumes excessive corporate tracking time.
