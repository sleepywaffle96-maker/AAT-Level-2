1. Core Internal Control Evaluation (ICE) Deficiency Matrix

The formal layout required to process, review, and report operational control failures during systems audits.

Identified Deficiency Location Specific Corporate Risk Exposure Recommended Control Policy Update
Purchase Ledger Permissions
Accounting clerks possess access rights to create new supplier entries and approve purchase invoices simultaneously.		 High fraud risk; a clerk could set up a fake supplier profile matching their personal bank details and authorize fraudulent payments to themselves. Enforce strict segregation of duties. The database must restrict clerk accounts so supplier configuration and invoice authorization are handled by separate staff.
Sales Despatch Reconciliation
Goods leave the shipping bays without verification against an authorized sales invoice or delivery note.		 Inventory shrinkage and loss of revenue; products could leave the facility unbilled, causing direct material losses. Configure a gating control. Warehouses must require a sequential system check where gatekeepers scan a Goods Despatch Note (GDN) that links directly to an active invoice.
Bank Reconciliation Pipeline
General bank ledger records are matched against statements only once every six months.		 Delayed fraud and error detection; unauthorized withdrawals or banking mistakes remain hidden for months, misstating liquid cash views. Enforce mandatory monthly bank reconciliations, compiled by an accounting team member independent of cash handling and signed off by a finance manager.
