Drafting & Interpreting Financial Statements (DAIF): Consolidated Financial Statements

AAT Level 4 Syllabus Context: This chapter covers the mechanical principles and standard regulations for preparing group accounts, calculating goodwill, and adjusting for intra-group trading under IFRS 3 and IFRS 10.

1. Group Structures and Control Parameters

When a limited company invests in another company, the relationship is determined by the percentage of equity shares held and the level of direct influence exercised over corporate decisions.

Subsidiary Companies (IFRS 10)

A subsidiary is an entity controlled by another entity (known as the Parent or Holding company). **Control** is generally presumed to exist when the parent company owns **more than 50%** of the voting equity shares. Under IFRS 10, control requires exposure to variable returns and the practical ability to affect those returns through corporate voting power. Parent companies must prepare consolidated financial statements to present the group as a single economic entity.

Associate Companies (IAS 28)

An associate is an entity over which the investing investor company possesses **significant influence**, but not full operational control. Significant influence is presumed when the investor holds between **20% and 50%** of the voting equity shares. Associates are not consolidated line-by-line; instead, they are accounted for using the **Equity Method** within the consolidated Statement of Financial Position (SoFP), appearing as a single asset entry called "Investment in Associates".

2. Consolidated Statement of Financial Position Adjustments

Group accounting requires replacing the parent company's original asset entry "Investment in Subsidiary" with the individual assets and liabilities of the subsidiary, combined line-by-line.

Goodwill Calculation (IFRS 3)

Goodwill represents the excess consideration paid by a parent company over the fair value of the subsidiary's net identifiable assets at the acquisition date. It represents intangible economic value such as brand reputation, customer loyalty, and systemic business synergy. Goodwill is capitalised as a non-current asset on the consolidated SoFP and checked annually for impairment under IAS 36.

Non-Controlling Interest (NCI)

When a parent company purchases less than 100% of a subsidiary's equity shares, the remaining shares are held by external investors. This portion is called the Non-Controlling Interest. The NCI represents the proportion of net assets and profits not owned by the parent company and is disclosed as a separate line item within the Equity section of the consolidated SoFP.

3. Elimination of Intra-Group Transactions

A group cannot make a profit by trading with itself. All financial relationships and transactions between the parent and subsidiary must be completely eliminated upon consolidation:

  • Intra-Group Receivables and Payables: Inter-company loan balances or outstanding trade balances must be cancelled against each other. They must not appear inside consolidated current assets or current liabilities.
  • Provision for Unrealised Profit (PUP): When goods are sold between group entities at a profit markup, and some of those items remain in closing inventory at year-end, the profit is unrealised from a group perspective. A PUP adjustment must be processed to reduce consolidated inventory values and group retained earnings.
  • Mid-Year Acquisitions: When a subsidiary is purchased part-way through an accounting year, its profits must be pro-rated across pre-acquisition and post-acquisition phases. Only post-acquisition earnings are included in consolidated group reserves.
