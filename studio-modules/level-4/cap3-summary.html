AMAC Control & Variance Cheat Sheet

Advanced mathematical equations for calculation checks during variance exams.

1. Planning vs Operational Matrix Verification

Material Price Planning Variance
(Original Standard Price - Revised Standard Price) x Revised Standard Quantity
Material Price Operational Variance
(Revised Standard Price - Actual Price) x Actual Quantity Purchased

2. Advanced Direct Labor Formulations

Labour Rate Variance
(Standard Rate - Actual Rate) x Actual Hours Worked
Labour Efficiency Variance
(Standard Hours for Actual Output - Actual Hours Worked) x Standard Rate

3. Mix and Yield Verification Mechanics

Mix Variance = (Actual Quantity in Standard Proportions - Actual Quantity in Actual Proportions) x Standard Price

4. Control System Management Core

  • Revised Standard: A baseline recalculation generated when an initial budget projection becomes unrealistic due to persistent external market forces.
  • Interdependence: A shift in performance in one sector directly affecting values in another (e.g., lower raw material pricing causing increased wastage rates).
  • Controllability Principle: The framework stating that managers must be evaluated strictly on variances that their direct actions can influence.
