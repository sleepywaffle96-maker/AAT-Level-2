AMAC Advanced Variance Formula Cheat Sheet

Essential formulas and mathematical algorithms for advanced operational control exams.

1. Planning vs Operational Variance Formula Split

Material Price Planning Variance
Planning Variance = (Original Standard Price - Revised Standard Price) x Revised Standard Quantity
Material Price Operational Variance
Operational Variance = (Revised Standard Price - Actual Price) x Actual Quantity Used

2. Material Mix & Yield Mechanics

Material Mix Variance
Mix Variance = (Actual Quantity in Standard Mix Proportions - Actual Quantity in Actual Mix Proportions) x Standard Price
Material Yield Variance
Yield Variance = (Standard Quantity for Actual Output - Actual Quantity Used in Standard Mix Proportions) x Standard Price

3. Direct Labour Variance Matrix

Labour Rate Variance
Labour Rate = (Standard Hourly Rate - Actual Hourly Rate paid) x Actual Hours Worked
Labour Efficiency Variance
Labour Efficiency = (Standard Hours allowed for Actual Output - Actual Hours Worked) x Standard Hourly Rate

4. Control System Revision Checklist

  • Controllability Principle: Operational managers must only be evaluated on variances that their direct decisions can influence.
  • Attainable Baseline: A standard target that builds in realistic allowances for normal material shrinkage, minor machine downtime, and human breaks.
  • Variance Interdependency: Occurs when a decision in one department directly impacts another metric (e.g., buying cheap materials causing an adverse labor efficiency variance).
