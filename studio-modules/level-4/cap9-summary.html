3. Critical Regulatory and Criminal Terms Checklist

  • Public Interest Duty: The overriding professional obligation requiring accountants to protect the integrity of financial systems and wider society, placing it ahead of client or employer loyalty.
  • NOCLAR (Non-Compliance with Laws and Regulations): The international accounting framework setting out an accountant's legal duty to report illegal client actions directly to external authorities.
  • Greenwashing: Disclosing exaggerated or misleading environmental statistics to present a false eco-friendly corporate image.
  • Tipping Off: Informing a client or suspect that they are under investigation for money laundering. This is a severe criminal offense under the Proceeds of Crime Act (POCA) 2002.
  • Suspicious Activity Report (SAR): The standard legal document an accountant must submit to the National Crime Agency (NCA) upon discovering indicators of money laundering.
