1. Ethical Conflict Escalation and Resolution Blueprint

The standardized structural workflow recommended by the AAT when an accounting professional identifies material financial malpractice or fraud.

[ STAGE 1: Internal Documentation ] ──► Accumulate evidence, check corporate policy channels.
                     │
                     ▼
[ STAGE 2: Internal Escalation ]    ──► Report to superior line manager or the independent Audit Committee.
                     │
                     ▼
[ STAGE 3: Professional Advice ]    ──► Contact AAT ethical helplines to clarify statutory parameters.
                     │
                     ▼
[ STAGE 4: Legal Consultation ]    ──► Seek independent legal advice under strict professional privilege.
                     │
                     ▼
[ STAGE 5: Final Recourse ]        ──► Resign from position if directors refuse to resolve the fraud.

2. Ethical Dilemma Case Analysis Matrix

The official case deconstruction grid mapping complex corporate challenges, identifying underlying threats, and applying valid safeguards.

Corporate Dilemma Scenario Primary Ethical Threat Target System Control / Safeguard Policy
The finance director asks you to delay recording a batch of trade payable invoices until next year to meet profit targets. Intimidation Threat (Fear of dismissal)
Self-Interest Threat (Target bonus pressure)		 Refuse to delay the logs, citing the matching principle and **Integrity**. Document the directive and report the scenario directly to the Audit Committee.
An auditor is asked to evaluate the internal compliance and control system of a company where they configured the database framework. Self-Review Threat (Auditing own configuration) Decline the specific audit engagement due to a compromise of **Objectivity**. Request an independent audit team handle the evaluation.
A procurement manager requests you backdate a purchase contract to a date matching a favorable currency exchange rate tier. Advocacy Threat (Aiding deceptive positions) Refuse the adjustment immediately, as backdating documents is illegal and violates **Professional Behaviour**. File an internal security note.
