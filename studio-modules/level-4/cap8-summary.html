IASC Risk Reference Sheet & Checklist

Essential parameters and defense rules for corporate risk management.

1. Risk Valuation Metrics

Risk Exposure Scoring Formula
Risk Exposure Score = Likelihood Probability Value x Impact Severity Value

2. The Core Risk Response Options

Avoidance: Exiting the risk-generating activity completely.

Reduction: Implementing internal controls to suppress likelihood or severity parameters.

Transfer: Shifting the financial burden to an external party (e.g., purchasing insurance, outsourcing operations).

Acceptance: Tolerating minor residual risks when defensive optimization costs exceed potential losses.

3. Critical Fraud Prevention Indicators

  • Teeming and Lading: A fraud method in which cash from a customer is stolen and covered up using subsequent payments from other customers.
  • Data Backups: System records must be backed up daily to independent, immutable off-site locations or isolated secondary clouds to ensure recovery during ransomware attacks.
  • Whistleblowing Infrastructure: Providing secure, anonymous, and legally compliant internal reporting lines to encourage staff to report fraudulent anomalies early.
