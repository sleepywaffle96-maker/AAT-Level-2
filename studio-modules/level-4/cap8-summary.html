IASC Risk Reference Sheet & Checklist

Essential definitions and threat indicators for advanced corporate risk management exams.

1. Strategic Risk Quantification

Risk Exposure Scoring Value
Risk Exposure Score = Likelihood Probability Rate x Impact Severity Value (£)

Use this score to rank and prioritize corporate capital projects and choose between avoidance or mitigation paths.

2. The Structural Defenses of the Fraud Triangle

Pressure: Managed through competitive remuneration tiers, fair personal performance scorecards, and strong employee welfare support systems.

Opportunity: Elliminated directly by designing robust segregation of duties, automated audit trail logs, and multi-signature bank limits.

Rationalisation: Addressed via clear ethical onboarding pathways, anonymous internal whistleblowing infrastructure, and an uncompromised "tone from the top".

3. Critical Fraud Prevention Indicators Checklist

  • Teeming & Lading Malpractice: A sophisticated theft pattern targeting receivable assets, concealed by shifting subsequent customer cash inflows to cover previous shortfalls.
  • Immutable Data Backups: System files must be automatically synchronized to off-site storage clusters that are completely separated from the primary network loop to secure ledger access during ransomware incidents.
  • Disaster Recovery Site (Hot Site): A fully operational backup facility that is pre-configured with the latest database mirrors, allowing a seamless swap and preserving continuity during a catastrophic infrastructure failure.
  • Whistleblowing Safeguard Laws: Corporate procedures must comply with UK legislation to guarantee full anonymity and complete employment protection for staff exposing fraud.
