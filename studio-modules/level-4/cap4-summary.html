DAIF Framework Cheat Sheet & Reference

Essential parameters and calculation constraints for accounting standard compliance checks.

1. Inventory Net Realisable Value Constraints (IAS 2)

Net Realisable Value Calculation
NRV = Estimated Selling Price - Costs to Complete Production - Costs to Market/Sell/Distribute

Compare this result directly against historical unit cost. Value the closing stock lines at the lower of the two figures.

2. Non-Current Asset Revaluation Adjustments (IAS 16)

Revaluation Surplus Equation
Surplus = Asset Market Valuation - Net Book Value at Date of Valuation
Excess Annual Reserves Transfer Calculation
Transfer Amount = New Depreciation Charge - Original Historical Cost Depreciation Charge

3. Asset Impairment Valuation Limits (IAS 36)

Recoverable Amount Condition
Recoverable Amount = HIGHER of (Fair Value Less Costs to Sell) and (Value in Use)

An asset is impaired if its carrying value exceeds this baseline. Write down the difference to the SoPL.

4. Corporate Reporting Core Rules Reference

  • Substance Over Form: Financial statements must reflect the economic reality of a transaction rather than its legal structure to ensure a faithful representation.
  • Constructive Obligation: Arises when an entity's past practices, established patterns, or published policies create a valid expectation among third parties that the company will accept and settle a liability.
  • Adjusting Event: Post-reporting-date evidence that confirms conditions already present at the year-end milestone date (e.g., a customer liquidating their business).
