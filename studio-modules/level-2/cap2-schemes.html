Error Taxonomy Classification Does it affect Trial Balance Equality? Is a Suspense Account opened?
Error of Omission (Transaction completely forgotten) No (Balanced) No Suspense Needed
Error of Commission (Wrong account, correct category) No (Balanced) No Suspense Needed

Single-Sided Entry Error (Debit posted without Credit) Yes (Imbalanced) Yes (Opened immediately)

4. Suspense Account Operational Clearance Model

The layout below details an active assessment scenario where the Trial Balance failed to equate, resulting in an initial short-term imbalance of £360 placed on the credit side. Two matching errors are later tracked and processed via the General Journal.

Identified Accounting Faults

  • Fault 1: A raw cash sales receipt of £400 was correctly processed inside the Cash Book but completely omitted from the Sales Revenue account in the General Ledger.
  • Fault 2: A purchase insurance bill of £40 was entered inside the Insurance Expense account as a credit entry by mistake, whilst hitting the Bank payments ledger correctly.

Rectification Journal Entries

Ref Account Affected Debit (Dr) Credit (Cr) Analytical Narrative
1 Suspense Account £400 Rectifying a single-sided omission by crediting Sales Revenue and debiting the temporary holding account.
Sales Revenue Account £400
2 Insurance Expense Account £80 Correcting a reversed posting by debiting Insurance twice (£40 to cancel error + £40 for true entry) and balancing via Suspense.
Suspense Account £80
