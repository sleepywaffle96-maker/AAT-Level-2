Chapter 1: Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

1. The Core Accounting Equation Relationships

Every single transaction entered into the General Ledger must maintain the equilibrium of the balance sheet equation. Use these variations depending on the objective target:

Standard Format
Assets = Liabilities + Capital
To Find Equity
Capital = Assets - Liabilities
To Find Debts
Liabilities = Assets - Capital

2. UK VAT Breakdown & Extraction Formulas

In the UK AAT examinations, you must quickly calculate tax fractions based on an standard rate of 20%. Use the following mathematical shortcuts to jump between values:

From Net to VAT Amount
VAT = Net × 0.20
  • Applies when starting from the core cost/revenue figure.
  • Example: £500 Net × 0.20 = £100 VAT.
From Net to Gross Total
Gross = Net × 1.20
  • Calculates the final invoice total due including tax.
  • Example: £500 Net × 1.20 = £600 Gross.
Extract VAT From Gross (Shortcut)
VAT = Gross / 6
  • The standard AAT exam formula to isolate output/input tax.
  • Alternative: (Gross / 120) × 20.
  • Example: £600 Gross / 6 = £100 VAT.
Extract Net From Gross Total
Net = Gross / 1.20
  • Removes the tax layer to find the true income or expense.
  • Example: £600 Gross / 1.20 = £500 Net.

3. The Double-Entry Engine & Petty Cash Controls

Core algorithmic posting rules and balancing criteria that must be memorised for instant recall during time-pressured assessments:

DEAD Accounts

Increases are recorded on the Debit side:

  • Expenses (Costs incurred)
  • Assets (Resources owned)
  • Drawings (Owner withdrawals)
CLIC Accounts

Increases are recorded on the Credit side:

  • Liabilities (Debts owed)
  • Income (Revenues generated)
  • Capital (Owner investment)
Petty Cash Imprest Control Equation
Imprest Float = Physical Cash in Hand + Sum of Paid Petty Cash Vouchers
  • Reimbursement Rule: The cash amount needed to top up the tin is always exactly equal to the total value of the spent vouchers.
  • Double Posting Check: Total credit sales gross value always hits the debit side of Trade Receivables; net value hits credit sales revenue; tax hits credit VAT control.
