Chapter 1 Cheat Sheet - Fast Tracking Matrix

Use this technical cheat sheet as an immediate classification template during assessments to correctly separate elements before launching calculations.

Assets Verification Checklist

  • Non-Current Asset: Freehold Premises (Property/Land).
  • Non-Current Asset: Motor Vehicles, Delivery Vans, Fleet.
  • Non-Current Asset: Machinery, Plant, Heavy Equipment.
  • Current Asset: Trading Inventory (Goods held for resale).
  • Current Asset: Trade Receivables Control Account (SLCA).
  • Current Asset: Bank Cash Deposits and physical Petty Cash fund.

Liabilities Verification Checklist

  • Non-Current Liability: Commercial Bank Mortgage.
  • Non-Current Liability: 5-Year or long-term operational Bank Loans.
  • Current Liability: Trade Payables Control Account (PLCA).
  • Current Liability: Short-term Bank Overdraft due on demand.
  • Current Liability: Value Added Tax (VAT) net balance owed to HMRC.

3. Critical Assessment Check: Legal Status Defenses

A standard trap in AAT Chapter 1 tasks is testing scenarios where a sole trader claims limited liability. Use this fast reference guide to identify legal realities instantly:

The Legal Realities Reference Framework

  • Sole Trader vs Ltd Debt Responsibility:
    If a Sole Trader business goes into liquidation, the owner is personally liable for 100% of the unpaid debts. If a Private Limited Company (Ltd) goes into liquidation, the shareholders are protected by limited liability and lose only their invested share capital.
  • Separate Legal Entity Status:
    Sole Traders and standard Partnerships are not separate legal entities from their owners. A Limited Company (Ltd) is a separate legal person in the eyes of the law, independent of directors and shareholders.
  • Public Accountability Requirements:
    Sole Traders do not publish accounts publicly. Private Limited Companies (Ltd) must file audited statutory financial statements at Companies House every single year for public inspection.
