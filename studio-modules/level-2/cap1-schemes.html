Chapter 1 Schemes & Visual Mappings

This section provides the essential visual frameworks and mapping diagrams required by AAT standards to fully trace corporate source documents and equation shifts.

1. The Document Pipeline Map (The Credit Purchase Audit Trail)

In a standard professional business sequence, data moves chronologically between departments via specific primary resources. Note the legal trigger point below:

1. Internal Purchase Requisition & Formal Purchase Order (PO)
Issued by Buyer to Seller — No Accounting Entry
▼
2. Supplier's Goods Delivery Note (GDN)
Accompanies physical stock — Used for Warehouse checking only
▼
3. OFFICIAL SALES / PURCHASE INVOICE
The Definitive Legal Document — TRIGGERS THE BOOKKEEPING RECORD ENTRY
▼
4. Credit Note (If required for returns or overcharges) or Statement of Account
Updates ledger profile balances and clears outstanding credit variances

2. Symmetrical Impact Map: Transactions vs Accounting Equation

Every commercial event modifies at least two fundamental variables inside the ledger framework. Use this master diagram map to trace how typical transactions balance symmetrically:

Commercial Transaction Type Impact on ASSETS side Impact on LIABILITIES side Impact on CAPITAL side
1. Owner introduces personal Cash into the business Bank account INCREASES (+ Bank) No Effect INCREASES (+ Capital)
2. Business secures a long-term commercial Bank Loan INCREASES (+ Bank) INCREASES (+ Non-Current Liability) No Effect
3. Business buys Equipment, paying instantly via Bank Debit Card NO GLOBAL EFFECT
(+ Equipment Asset / - Bank Asset)		 No Effect No Effect
4. Business purchases raw trading Inventory on Credit terms INCREASES (+ Inventory Asset) INCREASES (+ Current Liability: PLCA) No Effect
5. Owner withdraws Cash from business Bank account for personal use DECREASES (- Bank) No Effect DECREASES (- Drawings / Capital)
