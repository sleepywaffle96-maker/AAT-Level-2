Chapter 4 Cheat Sheet - Error Correction Matrix

Use this technical checkpoint layout as a systematic reference framework during timed simulations to instantly determine suspense and profit requirements.

Errors Where Trial Balance AGREES

  • Omission: Whole entry is completely missing from all nominal accounts.
  • Commission: Symmetrical posting into the wrong customer/supplier name.
  • Principle: Symmetrical posting into the wrong asset/expense class.
  • Original Entry: The correct accounts are used but with a matching wrong value.
  • Reversal: Debits and credits are fully swapped on both active sides.

Errors Where Trial Balance DISAGREES

  • Casting Error: Addition mistakes inside day books or cash columns.
  • Single-Sided Entry: Posting the debit side but completely omitting the credit.
  • Transposition: Reversing digits on one ledger profile only (e.g., £54 vs £45).
  • Double Posting: Debiting two separate nominal accounts with no credit entry.

3. Critical Assessment Check: Net Profit Adjustment Rules

A recurring trap in AAT journal assessments is miscalculating profit adjustments. Use this checklist to determine net income modifications instantly:

The Net Profit Adjustment Protocol

  • The Nominal Expense Rule:
    If a journal correction requires a DEBIT to an expense account or a revenue account, the net profit will DECREASE because overheads increase or turnover drops.
  • The Nominal Income Rule:
    If a journal correction requires a CREDIT to an expense account or a revenue account, the net profit will INCREASE because overheads decrease or sales turnover expands.
  • The Balance Sheet Exclusion Principle:
    If an adjusting entry only modifies Assets, Liabilities, Capital, or Suspense parameters (e.g., correcting an error of commission between two customer accounts), the company's Net Profit remains completely unaffected.
