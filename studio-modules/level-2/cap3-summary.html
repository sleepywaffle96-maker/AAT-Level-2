Chapter 3: Principles of Costing Cheat Sheet

1. Primary Mathematical Cost Formulations

Use these calculations to structure manufacturing totals and perform inventory appraisals:

Prime Cost Equation
Prime Cost = Direct Materials + Direct Labour + Direct Expenses
AVCO Inventory Pricing
Average Unit Cost = Total Value of Stock in Hand / Total Quantity of Stock Units

2. Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) Numerical Equations

These core equations are vital for resolving algorithmic simulator problems regarding production targets:

Contribution Per Unit
Contribution per Unit = Selling Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit
Total Contribution
Total Contribution = Contribution per Unit × Quantity Sold
Total Contribution = Fixed Overheads + Net Profit
Break-Even Sales Volume
Break-Even (Units) = Total Fixed Costs / Contribution per Unit
Margin of Safety Formulations
Margin of Safety (Units) = Budgeted Sales Units - Break-Even Sales Units
Margin of Safety % = (Margin of Safety Units / Budgeted Sales Units) × 100

3. Labour Remuneration and Inventory Control Rules

Operational principles for rapid validation checks within algorithmic testing modules:

Core Costing Checklist
  • Overtime Premium Allocation: Treated as an indirect cost (overhead) unless a client requests an emergency delivery, which shifts the premium into direct costs.
  • Idle Time Cost Allocation: Paid time with zero production output is always charged to Indirect Labour overheads.
  • Variable Cost Behaviour: Variable cost per unit remains entirely *constant*, while the total variable expenditure alters in direct proportion to production scale.
  • Fixed Cost Behaviour: Total fixed expenditure remains completely *constant*, while the fixed cost per unit declines steadily as production volume expands.
