Chapter 3 Cheat Sheet - Cash Controls Matrix

Use this technical checkpoint layout as a systematic reference framework during timed simulations to instantly isolate cash flows and voucher balances.

The DEBIT Side Checklist (Receipts)

  • Customer Receipts: Direct bank electronic transfers or cash settlements from trade clients.
  • Cash Sales: Immediate on-the-spot liquid sales turnover revenue.
  • Capital Injected: Funds introduced into the bank account by the business owner.
  • Contra Entries: Physical cash drawn from the office safe and deposited into the bank.

The CREDIT Side Checklist (Payments)

  • Supplier Payments: Outbound electronic payments sent to settle trade invoices.
  • Business Expenses: Wages, commercial rent, utilities, insurance settlements.
  • Owner's Drawings: Financial capital extracted from bank columns for private use.
  • Contra Entries: Funds withdrawn from bank columns to top up physical cash safe till.

3. Critical Assessment Check: The Imprest Protocol Realities

A recurring trap in AAT liquid fund assessments is failing to isolate discrepancy effects. Use this reference block to verify balancing calculations:

The Continuous Imprest Checking Matrix

  • The Fundamental Balancing Theorem:
    Physical Cash remaining inside the box + Combined Gross Value of all signed vouchers = The original fixed Imprest Float level. Any variance confirms a shortage or surplus.
  • The Reimbursement Rule:
    The bank top-up cheque required to restore the pool is always exactly equal to the total value of vouchers spent during the window. A cash shortage does not reduce the top-up cheque value; it opens a variance entry.
  • The Discrepancy Treatment Protocol:
    A cash shortage represents an unresolved financial loss and must be debited to an overhead expense account. A cash surplus represents an unexpected gain and must be credited to an income account to rebalance the ledger framework.
