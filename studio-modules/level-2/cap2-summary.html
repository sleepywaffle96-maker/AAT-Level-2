Chapter 2: Control Accounts & Reconciliations Cheat Sheet

1. Bank Reconciliation Algorithmic Math

Reconciling cash must always follow this strict two-stage logical flow to resolve numeric simulation tasks:

Stage 1: Update Internal Cash Book
Updated Cash Book = Original Balance + Direct Credits + Bank Interest - Bank Charges - Direct Debits - Standing Orders - Dishonoured Cheques
Stage 2: Bank Reconciliation Statement Target
Target Updated Cash Book = Bank Statement Balance + Outstanding Lodgements - Unpresented Cheques
  • Overdraft Note: If the bank statement is overdrawn, insert the starting statement balance as a negative figure.

2. Control Accounts Integrity & Contra Rules

Core reconciliation boundaries and ledger balances that must be memorised for instant recall during assessments:

SLCA Natural Balance

Classified as a Current Asset. Regular balance sits on the Debit side.

  • Reconciliation Check: SLCA Closing Balance = Total of Individual Customer Balances in Sales Ledger.
PLCA Natural Balance

Classified as a Current Liability. Regular balance sits on the Credit side.

  • Reconciliation Check: PLCA Closing Balance = Total of Individual Supplier Balances in Purchase Ledger.
Contra Entry Rule (Inter-Ledger Transfer)
Contra Value = Minimum(Customer Balance in SL, Supplier Balance in PL)
  • Double Posting Check: Always record the contra offset by making a Debit to PLCA (reducing liability) and a matching Credit to SLCA (reducing asset).

3. Error Correction & Suspense Diagnostics

Quick rules to isolate processing mistakes and track the temporary holding ledger account:

Trial Balance Agrees (No Suspense)

Errors that do not break the arithmetic balance because a debit matches a credit:

  • Omission: Left out entirely.
  • Commission: Correct type, wrong name.
  • Principle: Wrong account class (Asset vs Expense).
  • Original Entry: Inaccurate figure used on both sides.
  • Reversal: Inverted Dr/Cr positions.
Trial Balance Breaks (Suspense Opened)

Errors that create an unequal ledger balance and require immediate suspense investigation:

  • Single-Sided Entry: One part is missing.
  • Two Debits / Two Credits: Input on the same side.
  • Calculation Errors: Mismatched figures across ledger sides.
  • Target: Clear the balance to exactly £0.
