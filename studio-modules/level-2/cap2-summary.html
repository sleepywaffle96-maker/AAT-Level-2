General Ledger Debit Checkpoints

  • SLCA Total: Gross cumulative sum extracted from the Sales Day Book.
  • Purchases Total: Net cumulative sum extracted from the Purchase Day Book.
  • VAT Input Total: Recoverable VAT asset derived from the Purchase Day Book.
  • Sales Returns: Net cumulative returns from the Sales Returns Day Book.

General Ledger Credit Checkpoints

  • PLCA Total: Gross cumulative sum extracted from the Purchase Day Book.
  • Sales Revenue: Net cumulative turnover extracted from the Sales Day Book.
  • VAT Output Total: Collected tax liability derived from the Sales Day Book.
  • Purchase Returns: Net cumulative returns from the Purchase Returns Day Book.

3. Critical Assessment Check: Double-Entry vs Memorandum Realities

A standard trap in AAT Chapter 2 tasks is treating subsidiary records as double-entry components. Use this fast reference guide to identify technical realities instantly:

The Subsidiary vs Control Ledger Reference Framework

  • Double-Entry Balance Rule:
    The core double-entry matrix is satisfied entirely within the General Ledger using control accounts (SLCA and PLCA). Individual customer or supplier entries sit outside this loop.
  • The Memorandum Status Rule:
    The Sales Ledger and Purchase Ledger are strictly memorandum registers. They are utilized exclusively for credit tracking and customer relations, and their values never flow directly into the Trial Balance.
  • Trade Discount Calculation Order:
    Trade volume discounts must always be stripped out first from the catalog list price baseline before attempting any VAT multiplication. VAT is never applied to list price segments if an active trade reduction exists.
