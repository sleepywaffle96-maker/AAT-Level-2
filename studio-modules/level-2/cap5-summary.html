Chapter 5 Revision Cheat Sheet & Fast Checklist

Use this technical cheat sheet as an immediate verification matrix during assessments to isolate specific balance errors.

The DEBIT Side Checklist (DEAD)

  • Non-Current Assets: Premises, Vehicles, Equipment, Fixtures.
  • Current Assets: Inventory, Cash-in-hand, SLCA, positive Bank bal.
  • Purchases: Cost of raw goods acquired for business operations.
  • Expenses: Salaries, Rent, Rates, Insurance, Fuel, Marketing.
  • Drawings: Values taken by the owner for personal maintenance.
  • Sales Returns: Credit notes issued to customers returning goods.

The CREDIT Side Checklist (CLIC)

  • Capital Account: Initial and continuous investment by the owner.
  • Non-Current Liabilities: Bank Loans, Mortgages, long-term credit.
  • Current Liabilities: Trade Payables (PLCA), Bank Overdrafts, VAT due.
  • Sales Revenue: Turnover generated from primary credit and cash sales.
  • Purchase Returns: Credit allowances received from trade suppliers.
  • Discounts Received: Fin. benefits from paying suppliers early.

3. Deep-Dive: Errors NOT Revealed by a Trial Balance

A common trick in AAT assessments is testing situations where the columns match completely but the ledger is wrong. Below are the six types of errors that do not affect the totals:

The Invisible Errors Dictionary

  • 1. Error of Omission:
    A whole transaction is completely forgotten. No debit entry and no credit entry are created. Because both columns lack the number, the ledger balances remain equal.
  • 2. Error of Commission:
    A entry is made on the correct side and into the correct class of account, but into the wrong personal account profile (e.g., debiting the personal account of H. Green instead of H. Grey within the Sales Ledger).
  • 3. Error of Principle:
    A transaction is recorded on the correct side but into the completely wrong asset or liability class (e.g., debiting vehicle repairs expense inside the Motor Vehicles Asset Account instead of the Motor Expenses Overhead Account).
  • 4. Error of Original Entry:
    The double-entry layout uses the exact same incorrect starting number on both sides (e.g., recording a credit sale of £45 as £54 in both the SLCA and the Sales account). The columns stay balanced because the error amount is symmetrical.
  • 5. Error of Complete Reversal:
    The bookkeeping entries are applied backwards. The account that should have been debited is credited, and the account that should have been credited is debited. Symmetrical values keep the columns equal.
  • 6. Compensating Errors:
    Two completely separate, unrelated errors are made on opposite sides of the ledger that happen to be for the exact same amount. The debit mistake balances the credit mistake.
