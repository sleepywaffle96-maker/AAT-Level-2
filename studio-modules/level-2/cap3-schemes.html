Chapter 3: Cost Classification & CVP Analysis — Schemes

1. Prime Cost and Total Cost Building Blocks

The layout below details how individual direct and indirect components accumulate to construct a comprehensive corporate cost profile.

Prime Cost (Direct Accumulation)
Direct Materials + Direct Labour + Direct Expenses = Prime Cost
+
Production Overheads (Indirect Manufacturing Layer)
Indirect Factory Materials + Indirect Factory Wages + Factory Shared Utility Expenses
= Production Cost (Gross Cost of Product)
+
Non-Production Overheads (Commercial Support Layer)
Administrative Overheads + Distribution Costs + Marketing and Sales Expenses
= Total Cost of the Business Entity

2. Ledger Effects of Valuation Layouts (Inflationary Trends)

This comparison scheme maps out how picking an inventory framework alters financial outputs when raw goods purchase rates are rising.

Valuation Model Cost of Production Pricing Mode Closing Inventory Valuation Effect Net Profit Generation Impact
FIFO (First-In, First-Out) Lower (Uses older cheaper prices) Higher (Current market value) Higher reported operational profit
LIFO (Last-In, First-Out) Higher (Uses current expensive prices) Lower (Tied to historical stock costs) Lower reported operational profit
AVCO (Weighted Average) Blended mid-range pricing scale Averages all stock receipts together Smooths out short-term market spikes

3. Remuneration and Overtime Premium Classification

Overtime hourly pay contains two components. This matrix illustrates how to allocate basic rates versus premiums to keep cost structures precise.

Labour Payment Scenario Element Direct Labour Account Classification Indirect Labour Account (Overhead)
Basic hours worked by assembly line craftsmen 100% Allocated Here Excluded
Basic hours worked by factory clean-up crews Excluded 100% Allocated Here
Overtime Premium (General production run) Excluded Allocated as Factory Overhead
Overtime Premium (Customer urgent request) Allocated as Direct Cost Excluded
Idle Time Hours (Power cut disruption) Excluded Allocated as Factory Overhead

4. Cost-Volume-Profit Linear Relationship Model

This layout visualizes the accounting interaction of margins and fixed costs required to establish the operational break-even boundary.

Operational Volume Threshold Level Financial Performance Output State
Sales are *below* the Break-Even Point Total Contribution < Fixed Overheads (Net Loss State)
Sales are *at* the Break-Even Point Total Contribution = Fixed Overheads (Zero Profit/Loss Boundary)
Sales are *above* the Break-Even Point Total Contribution > Fixed Overheads (Net Profit State)
